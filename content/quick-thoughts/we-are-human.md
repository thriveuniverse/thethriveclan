---
title: We are human
---

An accountant comes back from holiday to find their substitute has made a mess of things. "It's a catastrophe," they say — not because the world's ending, but because they know exactly how much quiet work went into what's now undone, work they were probably proud of without ever saying so. We do this constantly. Language lets us just negate a sentence — like it, don't like it — but it also lets us reach further: incredible, gross, catastrophe. That reach comes from what we know and what we feel, not from the thing itself. This bias doesn't need removing. It needs noticing — because if you judge only by what's said, and never by what the speaker actually carries into saying it, you may be pulled toward the wrong conclusion.
