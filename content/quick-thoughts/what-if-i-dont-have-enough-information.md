---
title: What if I don't have enough information?
---

We can solve for one unknown. Maybe two. The real world doesn't stop at two — it has unlimited unknowns. Think of it like walking down a dark tunnel, seeing only a short distance ahead. Stay still, and eventually something reveals itself — a mouse crosses your path, and now you know there are more ahead. But take a step, and you get information the standing-still never would have given you. The question isn't whether you'll have enough information. It's whether you wait for something to hand it to you, or start walking and let the tunnel answer as you go. You may not like what you find. But once it's known, you can work with it — same as any equation.
